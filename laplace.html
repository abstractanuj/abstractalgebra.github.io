Laplace Transform of a Constant Function

The Laplace transform of a constant function f(t) = 1 is given by:

$$L\{f(t)\} = L\{1\} = \int_0^\infty e^{-st} dt$$

Evaluating the integral, we get:

$$L\{1\} = \left[-\frac{1}{s} e^{-st}\right]_0^\infty = \frac{1}{s}$$

Laplace Transform of t^n

Using the definition of the Laplace transform, we have:

$$ \mathcal{L}\{t^n\} = \int_0^{\infty} e^{-st} t^n dt $$

To evaluate this integral, we can use integration by parts:

$$ \begin{aligned} \mathcal{L}\{t^n\} &= \int_0^{\infty} e^{-st} t^n dt \\ &= \left[-\frac{t^n}{s} e^{-st}\right]_0^{\infty} + \frac{n}{s} \int_0^{\infty} e^{-st} t^{n-1} dt \\ &= \frac{n}{s} \int_0^{\infty} e^{-st} t^{n-1} dt \\ &= \frac{n}{s} \cdot \frac{n-1}{s} \int_0^{\infty} e^{-st} t^{n-2} dt \\ &= \frac{n}{s} \cdot \frac{n-1}{s} \cdot \frac{n-2}{s} \int_0^{\infty} e^{-st} t^{n-3} dt \\ &\vdots \\ &= \frac{n!}{s^{n}} \int_0^{\infty} e^{-st} dt \\ &= \frac{n!}{s^{n+1}} \end{aligned} $$

Therefore, we have found that the Laplace transform of $t^n$ is:

$$ \mathcal{L}\{t^n\} = \frac{n!}{s^{n+1}} $$
